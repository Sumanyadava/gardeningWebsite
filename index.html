FAQ

The watering frequency depends on various factors such as the type of plant, soil moisture level, weather conditions, and season. Generally, it's best to water plants when the top inch of soil feels dry to the touch. However, some plants may require more or less frequent watering, so it's essential to research the specific needs of each plant in your garden.

The ideal soil type varies depending on the plants you're growing and your local climate. In general, well-draining soil with a balanced mix of organic matter, such as compost or aged manure, is suitable for most garden plants. Conduct a soil test to determine its pH level and nutrient content, and amend the soil as needed to create optimal growing conditions.

Integrated pest management (IPM) practices, such as proper plant selection, crop rotation, companion planting, and maintaining good garden hygiene, can help prevent pest infestations and disease outbreaks. Regularly inspect your plants for signs of pests or diseases, and promptly address any issues with appropriate control measures, including organic pesticides or cultural practices.

Sustainable gardening practices aim to minimize environmental impact while promoting biodiversity and soil health. Key strategies include conserving water, reducing chemical inputs, composting organic waste, using native plants, and incorporating eco-friendly landscaping techniques such as rainwater harvesting and mulching.
